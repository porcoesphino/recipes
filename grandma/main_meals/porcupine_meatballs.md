# Porcupine Meatballs

This was one of Grandma Higgins recipes.

An old family favourite, we used to have it with mash and vegetables. Also nice with pasta, on its own or on a bread roll with cheese.

---

- *500 g* beef mince
- *1/2 cup* rice uncooked
- *1* onion small finely diced
- *1 tsp* salt
- *1 tsp* pepper
- *1 tablespoon* Tomato Sauce
- *1 tablespoon* Worcestershire Sauce
- *1* egg
- *1 tsp* dried mixed herbs
- *2* crushed garlic cloves
- *2* x 500g Rosella can condensed tomato soup. (any good brand will do)
- *1 1/4 cups* water
- *1* small chilli for added flavour in soup
- Flour to roll balls in before cooking.

---

1. Mix mince, rice, onion, egg, sauce, garlic, herbs and seasoning together in a bowl.
2. Shape into bite-sized balls.
3. Roll in flour.
4. Heat tomato soup and water until boiling, then drop in meatballs. (To get some added flavour add a small diced medium chilli to soup.)
5. Simmer for 45 minutes.

**Notes**: Tastes even better the next day. Freezes well.
